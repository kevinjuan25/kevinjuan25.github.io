Kevin Juan

I am a PhD student in Chemical and Biomolecular Engineering at the University of Pennsylvania in the Patel Research Group. We are part of the Penn Institute for Computational Science. My current research interest is in molecular dynamics (MD) simulations of antifreeze proteins found in species of fish, grass, and insects that inhabit the arctic regions. Specifically, I aim to use MD to study the binding mechanism and characteristics of antifreeze proteins that allow them to preferentially bind to ice in a vast excess of liquid water.

I graduated Magna Cum Laude from Cornell University in August 2019 with a bachelors degree in Chemical Engineering. During my time at Cornell, I was an undergraduate researcher for Professor Joo in the Department of Chemical and Biomolecular Engineering where I worked on electrospinning polymer nanofibers for cathodes in high peformance Li-Air batteries. I was also an NSF REU researcher at Drexel University with Professor Gogotsi where I developed thin-film, electrochromic microsupercapacitors using MXenes.

In addition to my undergraduate research, I was an active member of AguaClara, which is a student-led project team focused on developing sustainable water treatment plants for communities that lack access to conventional water treatment technology.
